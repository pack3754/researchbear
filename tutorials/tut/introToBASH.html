In the early days of GNU, there was one major stumbling block to the fledgling "free software" movement. The kernel (the core of the operating system, the piece that talks to hardware, schedules processes, etc) being used was a proprietary piece of software that was under strict licensing restrictions. What they really needed was to develop the OS from the ground up, beginning with this critical piece of software known as the kernel. Two factions rose to meet the challenge - one group headed by Richard Stallman
